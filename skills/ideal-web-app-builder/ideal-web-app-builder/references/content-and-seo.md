# Content and SEO

Use this before creating public pages, legal pages, blog content, metadata,
visual assets, diagrams, favicons, or OG images.

## Content Standard

Content should be true, specific, and appropriate to the market:

- no fake customer quotes
- no fake press logos
- no invented statistics
- no generic AI filler
- no empty blog categories
- no thin "coming soon" legal pages
- no vague claims without proof or qualification

Write complete pages at the length the subject deserves. Short is acceptable
only when short is the professional answer.

## Required Pages by Product Type

Most public products need:

- home or primary app entry
- pricing or plan explanation if monetized
- terms of service
- privacy policy
- contact or support
- security or data handling for B2B, health, finance, education, or developer
  tools
- status or incident path for operational products
- changelog or release notes when users need trust in ongoing updates

Marketing or SEO products usually need:

- blog index
- article template
- category/tag pages when useful
- author/source model
- diagrams or original visuals
- references and editorial angle

## Editorial Angle

Before writing blog content, define:

- target reader
- search intent
- competitor content gaps
- original point of view
- proof sources
- diagrams or visuals
- conversion path
- internal links
- update cadence

An article should be good enough that a domain expert would not dismiss it as
commodity content.

## Visuals and Diagrams

Use visuals that explain the product or subject:

- diagrams for architecture, workflows, comparisons, and decision trees
- product screenshots when the UI exists
- generated images only when they support the brand and content
- charts only when the data is real or clearly illustrative

Every important page should have a page-specific OG image. Blog posts should
have OG images that match the article, not one generic brand card.

## Metadata

For each important route:

- title
- description
- canonical URL
- Open Graph title, description, type, URL, image, dimensions, and alt
- social card metadata
- robots policy
- structured data when applicable
- sitemap inclusion decision

Next.js apps should use the framework metadata APIs rather than hand-writing
fragile head tags.

## Legal Pages

Terms and privacy pages must match the product:

- data collected
- accounts and user content
- payments and refunds
- acceptable use
- third-party processors
- cookies and analytics
- AI features and data handling when relevant
- security contact
- jurisdiction and dispute terms when needed

Use legal counsel for high-stakes products. A generated legal page is a draft,
not legal advice.
