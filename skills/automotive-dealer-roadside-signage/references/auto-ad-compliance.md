# Auto Ad Compliance

Load this file whenever the design mentions price, payment, APR, lease terms, or due-at-signing language.

This is not a substitute for legal review. It is the minimum knowledge needed to avoid building obviously risky dealer roadside creative.

## First principle

If the sign cannot carry the required finance or lease terms clearly and conspicuously, change the concept. Do not keep the concept and shrink the legal text.

## FTC truthfulness baseline

In March 2026, the FTC warned 97 auto dealership groups about deceptive pricing. The examples it highlighted included:

- advertising a price that omits required add-on costs
- misrepresenting which discounts actually apply
- using teaser payments or price claims that do not reflect real consumer obligations

That means the creative decision is upstream of layout. If the price or payment claim is not actually and usually available on the advertised basis, do not design around it.

Source:

- FTC warning press release, March 6, 2026  
  https://www.ftc.gov/news-events/news/press-releases/2026/03/ftc-warns-97-auto-dealership-groups-about-deceptive-pricing

## Closed-end credit ads: Regulation Z / CFPB §1026.24

For credit advertising, these are triggering terms:

- amount or percentage of any downpayment
- number of payments or repayment period
- amount of any payment
- amount of any finance charge

When one of those appears, the ad must also disclose:

- the downpayment amount or percentage
- the terms of repayment
- the APR, using the term "annual percentage rate" or "APR"

Important nuance from the CFPB text:

- "no downpayment" does not trigger the same way a positive downpayment amount does
- APR must be labeled as APR, not buried under another label
- if a payment stream changes or ends with a balloon payment, the repayment terms must reflect the full obligation

Practical design implication:

- High-speed boards are usually hostile to payment-led financing claims
- If the client insists on "Only $399/month" or "84 months," assume a disclosure redesign is required immediately

Source:

- CFPB Regulation Z, current §1026.24  
  https://www.consumerfinance.gov/rules-policy/regulations/1026/24/

## Consumer lease ads: Regulation M / CFPB §1013.7

For lease advertising, these triggering terms matter:

- the amount of any payment
- any capitalized cost reduction or other payment due before or at consummation or delivery
- a statement that no payment is required

When triggered, the ad must also disclose:

- that the transaction is a lease
- the total amount due before or at signing or delivery
- the number, amounts, and due dates or periods of scheduled payments
- whether a security deposit is required
- whether an extra charge may apply at lease end based on residual-value realization

Additional importance rules:

- references to components of amount due at signing cannot be more prominent than the total amount due
- do not describe a lease rate as an APR or equivalent term
- merchandise tags in the place of business have a special alternative disclosure path, but that does not rescue a highway billboard

Practical design implication:

- "$299/mo lease" on a roadside billboard is often the wrong medium
- event-led roadside creative plus compliant lower-friction media is frequently the better system

Sources:

- CFPB Regulation M, current §1013.7  
  https://www.consumerfinance.gov/rules-policy/regulations/1013/7/
- FTC guidance, "Advertising Consumer Leases"  
  https://www.ftc.gov/business-guidance/resources/advertising-consumer-leases

## Medium choice rules

If the message is:

- `event-led` -> billboard or frontage can work
- `inventory-led` -> billboard or frontage can work if the claim is true and simple
- `price-led` -> proceed only if truthful and the total claim can be understood clearly
- `payment-led` -> usually move to lower-speed or supporting media
- `lease-led` -> assume billboard risk until proven otherwise

## What to ask before approval

- Is the advertised deal actually and usually available?
- Are all required charges included or clearly accounted for?
- Does any payment claim trigger disclosures that the format cannot carry?
- Is the most prominent numeric claim the one the customer will really experience?
- Do state law, OEM co-op, or local dealer-ad rules add stricter requirements?

## Escalation boundary

This file is a screening tool, not legal signoff. If the client is staking campaign spend on a finance-led or lease-led roadside creative concept, have counsel or a dealer-ad specialist review it before production.
