# Roadside Legibility And Placement

Use this file when the problem is visibility rather than messaging taste.

## What matters first

Roadside signs are constrained by approach distance, traffic speed, lane changes, sign orientation, type size, contrast, and dwell time. Good copy cannot rescue a sign that is physically unreadable.

## Hard rules to start from

### 1. Design for detectability, then legibility, then comprehension

ISA frames effective signs as needing to be detectable, conspicuous, legible, and comprehensible. That ordering matters: if the sign is not seen early enough, the rest of the design is wasted.

### 2. Parallel wall signs underperform perpendicular signs

ISA notes that a sign mounted parallel to the roadway may need to be at least 70% larger than a perpendicular sign or it will not be readable in time. Treat wall-parallel storefront signs as weaker roadside tools unless scale or placement compensates.

### 3. Use distance-based type sizing, not desktop intuition

ISA's baseline recommendation is a minimum of 1 inch of letter height for every 25 feet of viewing distance for legal drivers. That implies:

- 410 feet -> about 16.4 inch letters
- 550 feet -> about 22 inch letters
- 680 feet -> about 27.2 inch letters

If the client wants fancy, narrow, script, or low-contrast lettering, increase size further or reject the type choice.

### 4. Match the sign to the road condition

ISA's sample legibility distances for roadside business visibility:

- 25-30 mph: 410 feet with lane change, 155 feet without lane change
- 35-40 mph: 550 feet with lane change, 185 feet without lane change
- 45-50 mph: 680 feet with lane change, 220 feet without lane change
- 55-60 mph: 720 feet with lane change, 265 feet without lane change

If the site does not provide that early read, solve with placement, scale, or secondary sign locations, not wishful design.

### 5. Digital boards are still static-message media

OAAA's digital issue brief says digital billboards typically rotate static images every 6 or 8 seconds and should not flash, scroll, or show full-motion video outside permitted contexts. Treat them as a sequence of static posters, not mini web pages.

### 6. Fewer message elements win

OAAA's best-practices guide reports Journal of Advertising Research findings that ads with two message elements are 21% more likely to be noticed than ads with five. For dealership work, that usually means:

- destination + one offer
- or offer + one action cue

Not destination + model + payment + APR + disclaimer + inventory count + phone number.

### 7. Use short copy as the default benchmark

OAAA calls "7 words or less" a proven benchmark for most OOH applications. This is not a law, but it is the right default until the placement clearly supports slower reading.

## Font-size heuristics from OOH practice

OAAA's billboard design ranges:

- 100-200 feet: 4-8 inch type
- 200-350 feet: 8-15 inch type
- 350-500 feet: 15-20 inch type
- 500-600 feet: 20-24 inch type
- 600+ feet: 24-40 inch type

Use this as a canvas sanity check alongside the stricter distance-based sizing method above.

## Electronic sign legibility nuance

The USSC Foundation computation summary uses a 30 legibility index and 40/60 letter-to-negative-space ratio as fixed values in its baseline formulas. FHWA's changeable-message-sign testing found:

- MUTCD's static-sign criterion uses 30 feet per inch of letter height
- for tested digital signs, people with normal vision achieved roughly 40-45 feet per inch
- for 20/40 vision, around 20 feet per inch was a more reasonable estimate

Important inference: do not take the best-case digital-board numbers and assume they hold for moving drivers in cluttered dealership corridors. Use conservative sizing.

## Placement notes for dealers

- `Pylon / monument`: prioritize store discovery and franchise identity
- `Frontage banner / window`: works when traffic slows or repeats exposure often
- `Highway bulletin`: brand, event, or destination cue only
- `Service-lane signs`: arrow clarity beats branding flourish
- `Digital roadside board`: good for rotating event bursts, inventory proof, and daypart swaps, but only if each frame stands alone
